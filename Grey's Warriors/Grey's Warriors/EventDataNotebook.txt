Russo-Turkish War^1877^Russia, Ottoman Empire, Romania, Serbia, Montenegro, Bulgarian Legion^The Russian-led coalition won the war, pushing the Ottomans back all the way to the gates of Constantinople, leading to the intervention of the western European great powers.
The Congress of Berlin^1878^Represented at the meeting were Europe's then six great powers: Russia, Great Britain, France, Austria-Hungary, Italy and Germany the Ottomans, and four Balkan states: Greece, Serbia, Romania and Montenegro.^The leader of the congress, German Chancellor Otto von Bismarck, sought to stabilise the Balkans, reduce the role of the defeated Ottoman Empire in the region, and balance the distinct interests of Britain, Russia and Austria-Hungary. He also wanted to avoid domination of the Balkans by Russia or the formation of a Greater Bulgaria, and to keep Constantinople in Ottoman hands.
World War II^1939^Joseph Stalin, Franklin D. Roosevelt, Winston Churchill Chiang Kai-Shek, Adolf Hitler and Bunito Mussolini^forming two opposing military alliances: the Allies and the Axis powers. In a total war directly involving more than 100 million personnel from more than 30 countries, the major participants threw their entire economic, industrial, and scientific capabilities behind the war effort, blurring the distinction between civilian and military resources.
